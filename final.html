The Final Years

Illness

in October of 2003, Steve Jobs was diagnosed with Pancreatic Cancer. In mid-2004, he announced his cancer to all of his employees at Apple. Stating he had a cancerous tumor in his pancreas.

Despite doctor's pleas, he resorted to alternative medicine for the first 9 months following his diagnosis. People from both sides of the issue, claim that Steve Jobs hitting the pause button on surgery to remove the tumor is what likely lead to his rapid decline in his health and ultimately his speedy death.

Steve Jobs finally had surgery, commonly referred to as the Whipple Procedure in July of 2004, which appeared to have removed the tumor successfully. He did not have any chemotherapy or radiation treatments.

Even as Steve Jobs' health rapidly declined, he continued to fulfil the duties of CEO. Steve Jobs formerly resigned as Apple's CEO in August of 2011, however he continued to work with Apple until the day he died. Tim Cook was named by Steve Jobs as his successor as CEO of Apple.

Death

Steve Jobs died at his home in Palo Alto, California around 3pm on October 5, 2011 from complications from pancreatic cancer which led to repritory arrest. Steve Jobs had lost conciousness the day before and was surrounded by his wife, children and sisters at the time of his death.

Apple, Microsoft, Disneyworld, and Disneyland flew their flags at half mast for nearly 2 weeks after his death. His death was announced to the public by both Apple and Pixar.

A small, private funeral was held on October 7, 2011. The Governor of California Jerry Brown announced that October 16 was Steve Jobs Day. A private memorial was held by Apple for its employees on October 19 at Apple's headquarters in Cupertino, CA.

At the time of his death, Steve Jobs' biological mother was in a nursing home suffering from dementia, she was not told of Steve's death. A video of the Apple Memorial Service is available on Apple's website.
