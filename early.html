The Early Life of Steve Jobs

Childhood

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955 to a Syrian father and a Swiss/German mother. However he was adopted as an infant to a couple named Paul and Clara Jobs.

Steve was constantly riddled with frustration towards formal school. In elementary school at Monta Luma, he was known for being a prankster. He attended Cupertino Junior High School, and then moved on to Homestead High School. It was here that he meet Steve Wozniak (a long time life friend) and his on-again-off-again girlfriend Chrisann Brennan. The couple split for good in 1977.

Atari & Spiritual Searching

Steve Jobs attended Reed College in Oregon from 1972 until the summer of 1973 when he moved back to California. In the Spring of 1974 he was working at Atari trying to save enough money to travel to India to seek spiritual enlightenment. Jobs left for India in the Summer of 1974 with Daniel Kottke and the first stop was intended to be Neem Karoli Baba, however his ashram was deserted because Baba died in the Summer of 1973. Kottke and Jobs then sought out Haidakhan Babaji, where they received the spiritual guidance they had been looking for. Jobs left India 7 months later. When he returned, Steve Jobs spent a time at a commune in Oregon called All One Farm

While Jobs was in India he experimented with several psychotropic drugs and refers to his use of LSD as One of the two or three most important things I have done in my life.

After leaving the commune in Oregon, Steve Jobs went back to work at Atari and was tasked with the completing the circuit board for the video game Breakout

Losing Apple

In April of 1985, John Sculley received authorization from the Board of Directors of Apple to remove Steve Jobs from all managerial duties except as Chairman. Steve Jobs believed that John Sculley was bad for Apple. After hearing of Steve Jobs' attempt to stage a boardroom coup against him, on May 24, 1985 Steve Jobs was officially relieved of all duties by the Board of Directors and John Sculley. Steve Jobs voluntarily resigned as chairman of Apple 5 months later.

In a 2005 commencement speech at Stanford University, Steve Jobs referred to his breakup with Apple as Being fired from Apple was the best thing to happen to me. It allowed me to enter into the most creative period in my life.
